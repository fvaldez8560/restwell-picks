Buying guide

How to choose a mattress

Start with how you sleep, not the spec sheet

The single biggest factor in picking the right mattress is your sleep position, not the foam density or coil count. Side sleepers generally feel best on a medium-soft surface that cushions the shoulder and hip so the spine stays level. Back sleepers usually want a medium feel that supports the lower back without letting the hips sag. Stomach sleepers tend to need something firmer, so the midsection doesn't sink and pull the spine out of alignment. If you're a combination sleeper who moves around at night, a medium firmness is the safest bet.

Foam vs. hybrid: what's actually different

All-foam mattresses contour closely to your body and isolate motion well, which is great if you share a bed and don't want to feel your partner roll over. Hybrid mattresses combine foam layers with an inner coil system, which adds bounce, edge support, and a bit more airflow. Neither is "better" across the board — foam tends to suit side sleepers and couples who want quiet, motion-free nights, while hybrids often suit back and stomach sleepers who want a more responsive feel.

Why buying a mattress online is actually worth it

It sounds counterintuitive to buy something you sleep on for a decade without lying on it first, but the model works in your favor. Cutting out the mattress showroom and the commissioned salesperson lets these brands sell the same quality materials for meaningfully less. And because every trial period runs well past the 30 nights your body needs to adjust to a new mattress, you get a longer, more honest test than any five-minute store visit could offer.

When to actually pull the trigger

Mattress pricing is fairly steady most of the year, but it dips hard around a handful of dates: Presidents Day, Memorial Day, Labor Day, and the Black Friday–Cyber Monday stretch. If your mattress isn't actively causing you pain or keeping you up at night, it's worth timing your purchase to one of these windows. Outside of them, most brands still run smaller ongoing promotions, so you're rarely paying full list price.
